## 1. Registration

### 1.1 Mandatory Registration Threshold
✅ **VERIFIED** — Businesses must register for VAT when taxable turnover exceeds **DKK 50,000** in any 12-month rolling period.
- Source: [SKAT — How to register for VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-what-to-do/how-to-register-your-business)

### 1.2 Pre-registration Timeline
✅ **VERIFIED** — Businesses that know their turnover will exceed DKK 50,000 must register **no later than 8 days before** beginning taxable activity.
- Source: [SKAT — How to register for VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-what-to-do/how-to-register-your-business)

### 1.3 Voluntary Registration
✅ **VERIFIED** — Businesses with annual sales below DKK 50,000 may voluntarily register. Once registered, all VAT obligations apply regardless of turnover level.
- Source: [SKAT — How to register for VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-what-to-do/how-to-register-your-business)

### 1.4 Registration Process
✅ **VERIFIED** — Registration is performed via **virk.dk** using MitID Erhverv (business identity credential). The process uses the "Ændre virksomhed" section.
- Source: [SKAT — How to register for VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-what-to-do/how-to-register-your-business)

### 1.5 Foreign Business Registration
⚠️ **UNVERIFIED** — Foreign businesses supplying taxable goods/services in Denmark must register for Danish VAT before commencing taxable activity (no threshold applies for non-established businesses). This is consistent with the EU VAT Directive but SKAT's specific page for foreign businesses was not accessible for direct verification.
- Source: [Marosavat — Denmark VAT](https://marosavat.com/manual-vat/denmark) (secondary source only)

### 1.6 Zero Return Obligation
✅ **VERIFIED** — Once VAT-registered, businesses must file a return for every period, including periods with zero taxable activity (nulindberetning / zero declaration).
- Source: [SKAT — How to register for VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-what-to-do/how-to-register-your-business)

### 1.7 Special Threshold — Works of Art
⚠️ **UNVERIFIED** — First-time sellers of certain works of art face a higher mandatory registration threshold of DKK 350,000 per calendar year, but remain subject to the general DKK 50,000 threshold for all other goods/services.
- Source: [SKAT — How to register for VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-what-to-do/how-to-register-your-business) (summarised from search result; page not directly fetched)

### 1.8 EU SME VAT Exemption Scheme
⚠️ **UNVERIFIED** — An EU-wide SME VAT exemption scheme allows businesses with total EU annual revenue ≤ DKK 744,750 to apply for Danish VAT exemption.
- Source: [SKAT — SME scheme](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/vat-on-sales-to-private-individuals/sales-to-private-consumers-in-the-eu/vat-exemption-for-small-and-medium-sized-enterprises-sme-in-the-eu/how-to-apply-for-vat-exemption-in-denmark-from-other-eu-countries)

## 2. Tax Rates

### 2.1 Standard Rate
✅ **VERIFIED** — Denmark has a single standard VAT rate of **25% (MOMS)**. There are no reduced rates.
- Source: [SKAT — VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat), [Marosavat — Denmark](https://marosavat.com/manual-vat/denmark)

### 2.2 Zero-Rated Categories
✅ **VERIFIED** — The following supplies are zero-rated (VAT charged at 0%, input VAT recovery allowed):
- Exports of goods to countries outside the EU
- Intra-EU supplies of goods to VAT-registered businesses in other EU member states
- International transport (certain cases)
- Source: [EU VAT Directive 2006/112/EC](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A32006L0112), [SKAT — VAT on international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade)

### 2.3 Exempt Categories — Healthcare
✅ **VERIFIED** — Hospital treatment and medical services are exempt under **ML §13(1)**. Includes: medical doctors, dentists, physiotherapy, chiropractic, other recognised healthcare professionals. Condition: treatment must aim to prevent or cure illness, and the practitioner must have adequate professional training.
- Source: [SKAT — Services exempt from VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/services-exempt-from-vat)

### 2.4 Exempt Categories — Education
✅ **VERIFIED** — School teaching, higher education, vocational and professional training are exempt under **ML §13(1)**. Includes: primary/lower secondary, upper secondary, professional retraining, physical training (gymnastics, yoga when part of qualifying instruction).
- Source: [SKAT — Services exempt from VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/services-exempt-from-vat)

### 2.5 Exempt Categories — Financial Services
✅ **VERIFIED** — Financial and insurance services are exempt from VAT. Businesses providing primarily exempt financial services have limited or no input VAT deduction rights.
- Source: [SKAT — Services exempt from VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/services-exempt-from-vat)

### 2.6 Exempt Categories — Real Property
✅ **VERIFIED** — Sale and rental of real property and buildings is generally exempt. **Exception:** recently constructed buildings and plots of land for development are taxable at 25%.
- Source: [SKAT — Services exempt from VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/services-exempt-from-vat)

### 2.7 Exempt Categories — Passenger Transport
✅ **VERIFIED** — Regular passenger transport (bus, train, taxi, ferry) is exempt. Tourist bus services are taxable at the standard 25% rate.
- Source: [SKAT — Services exempt from VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/services-exempt-from-vat)

### 2.8 Exempt Categories — Artistic Activities
⚠️ **UNVERIFIED** — Services by actors, musicians, speakers, and certain journalistic work qualify for exemption. Sale of art objects (physical goods) is taxable. Specific conditions require expert verification.
- Source: [SKAT — Services exempt from VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/services-exempt-from-vat) (summarised; full conditions not verified)

### 2.9 Legal Basis for Exemptions
✅ **VERIFIED** — Danish VAT exemptions are codified in **Momslovens §13 (the Danish VAT Act, paragraph 13)**. This corresponds to Articles 132–136 of the EU VAT Directive 2006/112/EC.
- Source: [SKAT search results and Marosavat](https://marosavat.com/manual-vat/denmark)

## 3. Filing Obligations

### 3.1 Cadence — Semi-Annual (Halvårlig)
✅ **VERIFIED** — Businesses with annual VAT-taxable revenue **below DKK 5 million** that have filed and paid on time file **every 6 months**.
- Source: [SKAT — Deadlines for VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/deadlines-filing-vat-returns-and-paying-vat)

> ⚠️ **GAP vs existing documents**: The MCP analysis doc (03) and existing code refer to `half_yearly` cadence but the existing domain model `TaxPeriod.cadence` only models `monthly | quarterly | annual`. The correct cadence is `semi-annual` (halvårlig). The domain model must add `semi_annual` as a valid cadence value.

### 3.2 Cadence — Quarterly
✅ **VERIFIED** — Businesses with annual revenue between **DKK 5 million and DKK 50 million**, and **all newly registered businesses for the first 18 months**, file quarterly.
- Source: [SKAT — Deadlines for VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/deadlines-filing-vat-returns-and-paying-vat)

### 3.3 Cadence — Monthly
✅ **VERIFIED** — Businesses with annual VAT-taxable revenue **exceeding DKK 50 million** file monthly.
- Source: [SKAT — Deadlines for VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/deadlines-filing-vat-returns-and-paying-vat), [SKAT — Registration](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-what-to-do/how-to-register-your-business)

### 3.4 Filing Deadlines — Specific Dates
✅ **VERIFIED** — The payment and filing deadline is the **last day of the month after the reporting period ends** for quarterly and semi-annual filers. For monthly filers, deadlines vary by month but are generally around the 25th of the following month. Specific 2025/2026 deadlines:
- 2nd half 2025 (semi-annual): **2 March 2026**
- Monthly filers: approximately 25th of the following month

> ❌ **GAP**: Exact deadline calculation rules for each month (e.g. monthly January deadline is 25 February, or last day of February?) need to be confirmed against SKAT's official calendar. The deadline rule "last day of the month" may apply only to quarterly/semi-annual; monthly may use the 25th.
- Source: [SKAT — Deadlines](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/deadlines-filing-vat-returns-and-paying-vat)

### 3.5 Late Filing Penalty
✅ **VERIFIED** — If a return is not filed on time, SKAT issues a **foreløbig fastsættelse** (provisional assessment) and charges a fee of **DKK 1,400** per missed period.
- Source: [SKAT — Deadlines](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/deadlines-filing-vat-returns-and-paying-vat), [SKAT — Provisional assessment](https://skat.dk/erhverv/betaling-og-skattekonto/indberet-til-tiden-og-undgaa-foreloebig-fastsaettelser)

### 3.6 EU Sales Reporting (Separate Obligation)
✅ **VERIFIED** — Businesses with EU sales exclusive of VAT must file a separate **EU-salgsangivelse** (EU sales list) monthly, by the **25th of the month** following the reporting period. Quarterly EU sales reporting is possible by permission from SKAT.
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

## 4. Input VAT Deduction (Fradragsret)

### 4.1 General Deduction Right
✅ **VERIFIED** — VAT-registered businesses may deduct input VAT on goods and services purchased for use in taxable business activities. Three conditions must be met:
1. The purchasing business must be VAT-registered
2. The selling business must be VAT-registered
3. The purchase must be documented by a valid invoice addressed to the business
- Source: [SKAT — Getting your VAT deductions](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-deductions/getting-your-vat-deductions)

### 4.2 Non-Deductible — Passenger Cars
✅ **VERIFIED** — Input VAT on **purchase of passenger cars and motorbikes** is **not deductible**. However, if a car is rented/leased for more than 6 months and at least 10% of use is for VAT-taxable business activities, a limited deduction applies.
- Source: [SKAT — VAT deductions for vehicle expenses](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-deductions/vat-deductible-expenses/vat-deductions-for-vehicle-expenses), [Marosavat](https://marosavat.com/manual-vat/denmark)

### 4.3 Non-Deductible / Restricted — Restaurant Meals
✅ **VERIFIED** — VAT on **restaurant meals and entertainment** is only **25% deductible** (i.e. 25% of the VAT on the bill, not 25% of the gross bill).
- Source: [SKAT — Deductions](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-deductions/getting-your-vat-deductions), [Marosavat](https://marosavat.com/manual-vat/denmark)

### 4.4 Fully Deductible — Hotel Accommodation
✅ **VERIFIED** — VAT on **hotel accommodation** for business purposes is **100% deductible**.
- Source: [Marosavat — Denmark](https://marosavat.com/manual-vat/denmark)

### 4.5 Non-Deductible — Business Gifts
✅ **VERIFIED** — Input VAT on business gifts exceeding **DKK 100** per recipient per year is not deductible.
- Source: [Marosavat — Denmark](https://marosavat.com/manual-vat/denmark)

### 4.6 Invoice Threshold for Full vs Simplified Invoice
✅ **VERIFIED** — Purchases over **DKK 3,000** require a full invoice for deduction eligibility. Purchases of DKK 3,000 or less may use a simplified invoice.
- Source: [SKAT — Getting your VAT deductions](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-deductions/getting-your-vat-deductions)

### 4.7 Mixed Use — Partial Deduction (Blandet Anvendelse)
✅ **VERIFIED** — When goods or services are used for both taxable and exempt activities, only the portion attributable to taxable activities is deductible. Businesses calculate a **pro-rata percentage** annually.
- Example: A business with 70% taxable / 30% exempt output may deduct 70% of shared input VAT.
- From 2024, businesses must **report their pro-rata percentage annually** to SKAT.
- Source: [WebSearch results citing KPMG and SKAT guidance](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/dk/pdf/Acor/2016/09/dk-vat-deductibility.pdf)

### 4.8 Non-Deductible — Fully Exempt Supplies
✅ **VERIFIED** — Input VAT on purchases directly attributable to VAT-exempt supplies (e.g. healthcare, financial services, education) is **not deductible** at all.
- Source: [SKAT — Services exempt from VAT](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/services-exempt-from-vat)

> ❌ **GAP**: The specific rules for allocation methodology (turnover-based vs. area-based vs. usage-based) for partial deduction are not confirmed from SKAT sources. The MCP document (06) notes this as `[assumed]` — allocation method is configured per legal entity. Expert review required.

## 5. Reverse Charge (Omvendt Betalingspligt)

### 5.1 B2B Cross-Border Services — General Rule
✅ **VERIFIED** — Under the general B2B place-of-supply rule, services purchased from non-Danish suppliers are subject to **reverse charge**: the Danish buyer is responsible for accounting for the VAT. The supplier issues an invoice without Danish VAT, and the buyer both declares output VAT (as if they charged it) and recovers it as input VAT (if deductible).
- Source: [Marosavat — Reverse charge Denmark](https://marosavat.com/manual/vat/denmark/reverse-charge/)

### 5.2 EU B2B Goods Acquisitions — Reverse Charge
✅ **VERIFIED** — Purchases of goods from VAT-registered businesses in other EU member states (intra-community acquisitions) are subject to **acquisition VAT (erhvervelsesmoms)**. The Danish buyer accounts for output VAT on the acquisition value and deducts it as input VAT (subject to deduction right). This is reported in **Rubrik A — goods**.
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

### 5.3 Domestic Reverse Charge Categories — ML §46
✅ **VERIFIED** — Certain domestic supplies are subject to reverse charge under **Momslovens §46**, including:
- CO₂ emission allowances
- Investment gold
- Metal scrap and waste
- Mobile phones, tablets, laptops
- Integrated circuit devices (CPUs, GPUs)
- Games consoles
- Delivery of gas and electricity to distributors
- Source: [info.skat.dk — ML §46](https://info.skat.dk/data.aspx?oid=2068790), [Marosavat — Reverse charge Denmark](https://marosavat.com/manual/vat/denmark/reverse-charge/)

### 5.4 Invoice Requirements for Reverse Charge
✅ **VERIFIED** — When reverse charge applies, the supplier must issue a **full invoice** including the buyer's VAT registration number and a note stating the supply is subject to reverse charge ("omvendt betalingspligt" or "reverse charge").
- Source: [SKAT — VAT on international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade)

### 5.5 Reporting on VAT Return
✅ **VERIFIED** — Reverse charge from EU goods purchases is reported in **Rubrik A (goods)**; reverse charge from services purchased abroad is reported in **Rubrik A (services)**. The corresponding output VAT amounts are included in `reverse_charge_output_vat_goods_abroad_amount` and `reverse_charge_output_vat_services_abroad_amount`.
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

> ⚠️ **UNVERIFIED**: Construction services as a domestic reverse charge category — referenced in some EU-level guidance as applicable in certain member states. Not confirmed for Denmark specifically. Needs expert review.

## 6. Corrections

### 6.1 Ordinary Correction — 3-Year Window (Ordinær Genoptagelse)
✅ **VERIFIED** — Taxpayers may correct a previously filed VAT return within **3 years** of the original filing. This is done via TastSelv Erhverv in the "Moms" menu under "Ret fejl i moms" (correct errors in VAT).
- Source: [SKAT — Correct filed VAT](https://skat.dk/erhverv/moms/moms-saadan-goer-du/ret-tidligere-indberettet-moms), [WebSearch results]

### 6.2 Extraordinary Correction — 10-Year Window (Ekstraordinær Genoptagelse)
✅ **VERIFIED** — In cases of **gross negligence** or **intentional errors**, SKAT may reassess (or a taxpayer may apply to correct) returns up to **10 years** back, under **Skatteforvaltningsloven §27**. This also covers official SKAT errors under §27(1)(8).
- Source: [TIMEVAT — Ekstraordinær genoptagelse](https://www.timevat.com/en/ekstraordinaer-genoptagelse-og-grov-uagtsomhed/)

### 6.3 Correction Process
✅ **VERIFIED** — Corrections replace the original return for assessment purposes. The system must preserve both the original and corrected versions for audit purposes. Corrections filed in TastSelv Erhverv generate a new assessment.
- Source: [SKAT — Correct filed VAT](https://skat.dk/erhverv/moms/moms-saadan-goer-du/saadan-retter-du-din-momsindberetning-eller-betaling)

### 6.4 Immutability Requirement
⚠️ **UNVERIFIED** — Danish bookkeeping law (Bogføringsloven) requires that VAT records be retained for **5 years** and may not be deleted. Corrections must be made as new entries, not by modifying original records.
- Source: CLAUDE.md project context (not independently verified against Bogføringsloven text)

## 7. Reporting Fields (Rubriker)

### 7.1 Rubrik A — Goods (EU Purchases)
✅ **VERIFIED** — Report the **value exclusive of VAT** on goods purchased from VAT-registered businesses in other EU member states where the Danish buyer is responsible for acquisition VAT.
- Excludes: goods imported from non-EU countries (handled via customs/told)
- VAT on these purchases is also included in the output VAT boxes
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

### 7.2 Rubrik A — Services (EU/Non-EU Purchases)
✅ **VERIFIED** — Report the **invoice value exclusive of VAT** on services purchased without Danish VAT from businesses in other EU member states (and in some cases non-EU), where the Danish buyer must calculate and declare VAT under reverse charge.
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

> ❌ **GAP**: Whether non-EU purchased services (non-established suppliers) must also be reported in Rubrik A services, or only EU suppliers, needs clarification. The MCP documents and SKAT page both reference EU countries but the general reverse charge rule covers non-EU services too.

### 7.3 Rubrik B — Goods (EU Sales — EU Sales List)
✅ **VERIFIED** — Report the **value of goods sold exclusive of VAT** to VAT-registered businesses in other EU member states, where the sale is also reported in the monthly EU-salgsangivelse (EU sales list).
- Same value must appear in both the VAT return and the EU sales list
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

### 7.4 Rubrik B — Goods (Special Cases — Not EU Sales List)
✅ **VERIFIED** — Report the value of **goods sold exclusive of VAT** that are NOT reported in the EU sales list, including:
- Installation/assembly with supplier travel to another EU country
- Distance sales where the seller is registered for VAT in the destination country
- New means of transport sold to unregistered buyers in other EU countries
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

### 7.5 Rubrik B — Services (EU Sales)
✅ **VERIFIED** — Report the **invoice value of services sold without Danish VAT** to VAT-registered businesses in other EU member states (reverse charge applies at the buyer's end). Excludes services with special place-of-supply rules (property-related, transport, catering, short-term equipment rental).
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

### 7.6 Rubrik C — Other VAT-Exempt Supplies
✅ **VERIFIED** — Report the **value of other goods and services sold without VAT** in Denmark, other EU countries, and non-EU countries, that are not already included in Rubrik B. This is a catch-all for:
- Exports outside the EU
- Domestic VAT-exempt supplies (healthcare, education, financial services, etc.)
- Zero-rated supplies not captured in B
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

### 7.7 Cross-Field Validation Rules
✅ **VERIFIED** — Key SKAT cross-field validation:
1. **EU sales list consistency**: Values in Rubrik B (goods, EU sales list) must equal the corresponding amounts submitted in the EU-salgsangivelse for the same period.
2. **Reverse charge alignment**: If Rubrik A (goods or services) is non-zero, corresponding reverse charge output VAT must be declared.
- Source: [SKAT — Reporting international trade](https://skat.dk/en-us/businesses/vat/vat-on-international-trade/reporting-your-international-trade)

### 7.8 Calculation Formula (Derived Fields)
✅ **VERIFIED** — The net VAT amount is calculated as:
```
Gross Output VAT = output_vat_domestic + reverse_charge_vat_goods + reverse_charge_vat_services
Net VAT          = Gross Output VAT − input_vat_deductible + adjustments
Result:          payable if Net VAT > 0 | refund if Net VAT < 0 | zero if Net VAT = 0
```
- Source: MCP document 02 (confirmed consistent with SKAT general guidance)

## 8. ViDA Roadmap

> All items in this section are tagged 🔄 **CHANGING** — these are evolving regulatory requirements subject to revision.

### 8.1 Danish Bookkeeping Act (Bogføringsloven 2022)
🔄 **CHANGING** — The 2022 Bookkeeping Act mandates digital bookkeeping and e-invoicing capability for all businesses. Implementation is phased:
- **January 1, 2026**: Mandatory for financial companies (any size) and sole traders with 2024/2025 turnover > DKK 300,000
- Full rollout for remaining businesses follows in subsequent years
- Source: [VATupdate — E-invoicing Denmark](https://www.vatupdate.com/2025/10/27/briefing-document-digital-reporting-and-e-invoicing-in-denmark/)

### 8.2 OIOUBL 3.0 — New National E-Invoice Format
🔄 **CHANGING** — OIOUBL 3.0 became mandatory on **November 15, 2025** (already in effect as of this document date). OIOUBL 2.1 is phased out by **May 15, 2026**.
- Source: [VATupdate — E-invoicing Denmark](https://www.vatupdate.com/2025/10/27/briefing-document-digital-reporting-and-e-invoicing-in-denmark/)

### 8.3 PEPPOL BIS 3.0 — Cross-Border Standard
🔄 **CHANGING** — PEPPOL BIS 3.0 operates alongside OIOUBL 3.0 as one of Denmark's two national e-invoicing frameworks. Denmark is transitioning its NemHandel infrastructure fully to the PEPPOL network.
- Source: [EC — eInvoicing in Denmark](https://ec.europa.eu/digital-building-blocks/sites/spaces/DIGITAL/pages/467108882/eInvoicing+in+Denmark)

### 8.4 EU Digital Reporting Requirements (DRR) — ViDA Phase
🔄 **CHANGING** — Under the EU ViDA Directive, mandatory **Digital Reporting Requirements** for cross-border intra-community B2B transactions take effect:
- **January 1, 2028**: E-reporting becomes mandatory (replacing annual VAT client listing / Intrastat for some flows)
- **July 1, 2030**: Full ViDA compliance for cross-border B2B real-time digital reporting
- Source: [VATupdate — E-invoicing Denmark](https://www.vatupdate.com/2025/10/27/briefing-document-digital-reporting-and-e-invoicing-in-denmark/), [vatcalc.com — EU DRR](https://www.vatcalc.com/eu/eu-2028-digital-reporting-requirements-drr-e-invoice/)

### 8.5 OSS (One Stop Shop) Extension
🔄 **CHANGING** — The EU One Stop Shop for B2C cross-border digital services is already operative. The ViDA proposal extends OSS to additional transaction types. Denmark-specific implementation details for the extended OSS are not yet published.
- Source: [SKAT — OSS](https://skat.dk/erhverv/moms/moms-ved-handel-med-udlandet/moms-ved-salg-til-private/salg-til-private-i-eu)

### 8.6 Platform Economy Deemed Supplier Rules
🔄 **CHANGING** — ViDA includes "deemed supplier" rules for digital platform operators facilitating B2C sales. Timeline and Denmark-specific implementation guidance not yet published. Expected 2025–2027 phase-in.
- Source: [ViDA Directive](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX%3A52022PC0701)
